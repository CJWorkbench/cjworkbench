Comparing numbers

Introduction

Historical dollar amounts cannot be directly compared with dollars today, because all prices slowly change over time, due to inflation. Inflation is the rate of increase in prices over a given period of time. In this lesson you'll learn how to compare amounts from different years using data from the bureau of labor statistics.

Using CPI

In order to convert an old dollar amount into a current dollar amount, we need to:
First, divide the old dollar amount by the CPI for that year
Then, multiply it by the CPI for the current year.
To do this, we need first to join our two tables.

Since both tables have a year variable, we’ll join using the Year.

Joining tables

There is a close relationship between ratios and percentages. A percentage is simply a fraction (a ratio) multiplied by 100:

=(X/Y)*100

Instructions

    The Join module takes each available value in the ‘Year’ column of the Teacher salaries’ table and finds the corresponding row in the CPI table.

    Joining is a powerful step that you can use to build datasets using tables from various sources, each containing different information. By bringing them together, the value of that information increases significantly.

Adjusting for inflation

The formula for converting an old dollar amount (Amt1) into an equivalent current dollar amount (Amt2) is:

Amt2=(Amt1/CPI1)*CPI2
In other words:
First, divide the old dollar amount by the CPI for that year. Then, multiply it by the CPI for the current year.

Instructions

  1. In step 3, Add Formula and type in:

    Once adjusted for inflation, we can see that the salaries of teachers in public elementary and secondary schools, in the U.S, had decreased from 2009 to 2016, which was not apparent in the original table.
