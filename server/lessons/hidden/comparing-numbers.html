Comparing numbers

Introduction

Is $100,000 large or small? It could be a big number in a city budget, or it could be a tiny number in a national budget. Most numbers don’t mean very much without some sort of comparison. The simplest types of comparisons are subtraction and division.

Difference

Let’s ask: How many people arrived or left each county between 2010 and 2017? We need to calculate the difference in population, which is a subtraction.

Instructions

  1. In step 3, add Formula.
  2. Subtract 2017 to 2010 by typing in the formula
    =(A7-A1)
    and name the new column pop difference.
  3. Name the new column population difference.

    A positive numbers means that the population increased, while a negative number means that the county’s population decreased.

  4. Sort the resulting column in increasing order.

Ratio

Let’s ask: Relative to its size, which county has lost or gained the most population? In other word: How many habitant in 2017 compared to the number of inhabitants in in 2010? The formula to calculate ‘How many (X) per (Y)’ is:

=(X/Y)

Instructions

  1. In step 8, add a new Formula and type in
    =(D1/C1)

    Numbers greater than 1 mean the population increased. Less than 1 means that the population decreased. A ratio of 2 would mean that the population doubled.

Ratio

There is a close relationship between ratios and percentages. A percentage is simply a fraction (a ratio) multiplied by 100:

=(X/Y)*100

Instructions

  1. In step 10, add a Calculate step. Chose X is what percent of Y, and select Pop estimate 2017 as X, and Pop estimate 2010 as Y.

    One advantage of percentages is to allow us to better grasp numbers: In 2017, the population of Concho county was 66.6% the size it was in 2010.

    The numbers in this new column are similar, just multiplied by 100. So 0.56 becomes 56% and so on.

Calculating Percentage Change

Percentage change is the difference between old and new numbers, expressed as a percentage of the old number. The formula to calculate the percentage change between Old and New is:

=((New - Old) / Old)* 100

Instructions

  1. In step 12, add another Calculate step and choose Percentage change.
  2. Compare 2017 to 2010.

    100% means the value doubled, 0% means it stayed the same, and a -50% change means the new number is half of the old one.

    Percentage change allows us to editorialize our numbers in yet a new way: ‘From 2010 to 2017, Concho county lost 33.7% of its population’.
