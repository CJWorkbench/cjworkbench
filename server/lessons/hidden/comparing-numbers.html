Comparing numbers

Introduction

Is $100,000 large or small? It could be a big number in a city budget, or it could be a tiny number in a national budget. Most numbers don’t mean very much without some sort of comparison.

In this lesson we will use data from the United States Census bureau to learn three common types of comparisons: subtraction, division, and percentage change.

Difference

Let’s ask: Which county had the greatest population decrease from 2010 to 2017? We need to calculate the difference in population, which is a subtraction.

Instructions

  1. Add Formula.
  2. Enter the formula
    =D1-C1
    and name the new column Pop difference.
  3. Name the new column population difference.

    A positive numbers means that the population increased, while a negative number means that the county’s population decreased.

  4. Sort the resulting column in increasing order.

    The first row will have the most negative number, meaning the county with greatest population loss.

Ratio

Larger counties are likely to have a greater population change. So let’s ask the question a different way: Relative to its size, which county has lost or gained the most population? In other words: How many people in 2017 compared to the number of people in 2010? We can accomplish this with a simple division, producing a ratio.

As we saw before, the formula to calculate ‘How many (X) per (Y)’ is:

=(X/Y)

Instructions

    =(D1/C1)

Percentage

There is a close relationship between ratios and percentages. A percentage is simply a fraction (a ratio) multiplied by 100:

=(X/Y)*100

Workbench includes an easy way to calculate percentages: the Calculate step.

=(X/Y)*100

Instructions

  1. Add a Calculate step. Chose X is what percent of Y, and select Pop estimate 2017 as X, and Pop estimate 2010 as Y.

    One advantage of percentages is to allow us to better grasp numbers: In 2017, the population of Concho county was 66.6% the size it was in 2010.

    The numbers in this new column are similar, just multiplied by 100. So 0.56 becomes 56% and so on.

Calculating Percentage Change

Percentage change is the difference between old and new numbers, expressed as a percentage of the old number. The formula to calculate the percentage change between Old and New is:

=((New - Old) / Old)* 100

You can enter this into a Formula step, or Calculate can compute this directly.

Instructions

  1. Add another Calculate step and choose Percentage change.
  2. Compare 2017 to 2010.

    100% means the value doubled, 0% means it stayed the same, and a -50% change means the new number is half of the old one.

    Percentage change allows us to describe our numbers in a new way: ‘From 2010 to 2017, Concho county lost 33.7% of its population’.
