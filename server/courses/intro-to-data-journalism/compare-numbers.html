Comparing numbers

Learn three common types of comparisons: difference, ratio, and percentage change.

Introduction

Is $1,000,000 large or small? It could be a big number in a city budget, or it could be a small one in a national budget. Most numbers don’t mean very much without some sort of comparison.

In this lesson we will use data from the United States Census bureau to learn three common types of comparisons: difference, ratio, and percentage change.

Data

This Census data is published by the United States Census bureau here.

The census happens every 10 years. The last one was in 2010, so every year since then is an estimate based on more recent surveys.

Difference

Let’s ask: 'Which county had the greatest population decrease from 2010 to 2017?' We need to calculate the difference in population, which is a simple subtraction.

Instructions

  1. Add Formula
  2. Type in the following formula:
    =(D1-C1)
  3. Name the new column Difference.

    A positive numbers means that the population increased, while a negative number means that the county’s population decreased.

  4. Sort the resulting column in ascending order.

    The first row will display the largest negative number, meaning the county with the greatest population loss.

Ratio

Larger counties are likely to have a greater population change. Let’s reframe:
'Relative to its size, which county has lost or gained the most population?
In other words: 'How many people in 2017 compared to the number of people in 2010?'

The formula to calculate ‘How many (X) per (Y)’ is a simple division, also called a ratio:

=(X/Y)

Instructions

  1. Insert a new Formula in step 4 (keep the previous one) and type in:
    =(D1/C1)
  2. Name the new column Ratio

    A ratio of 1 means that for every person in 2010, there is exactly 1 person in 2017.

    Numbers greater than 1 mean the population increased. Less than 1 means the population decreased. A ratio of 2 would mean that the population doubled.

Percentage

There is a close relationship between ratios and percentages. A percentage is simply a fraction (a ratio) multiplied by 100:

=(X/Y)*100

Instructions

  1. Add another Formula and type in:
    =(D1/C1)*100
  2. Name the new column Percentage

    The numbers in this new column are obviously similar to the Ratio column - just multiplied by 100.
    The advantage of percentages is to allow us to better grasp numbers: 'In 2017, the population of Concho county was 66.6% the size it was in 2010.'

Calculating Percentage Change

Percentage change is the difference between old and new numbers, expressed as a percentage of the old number.

The formula to calculate the percentage change between and Old and New numbers is:

=((New - Old) / Old)* 100

Instructions

  1. Add another Formula step and this time apply the percentage formula:
    =((D1 - C1) / D1)* 100

    Name this new column Percentage change

    A result of 100% means the value doubled, 0% means it stayed the same, and a -50% change means the new number is half the old one.

    Percentage change allows us to describe our numbers in yet a new way:
    ‘From 2010 to 2017, Concho county lost 33.7% of its population’.
